Dual Sentimental Analysis

The research project is a comprehensive analysis of 3.34GB of textual data containing both positive and negative sentiments, employing advanced techniques to extract valuable insights. Utilizing liblinear and Support Vector Machines (SVM), a robust sentiment analysis model is crafted. To mitigate polarity shift, a unique approach is implemented using the reverse review pair methodology, dividing the data into an 80% training set and a 20% testing set. Through iterative experimentation and analysis, traditional bag of words models is refined, achieving an impressive accuracy rate of 82.7% in sentiment classification.

Green Mills Aggregate Planning Analysis

A business strategic initiative to address rising raw wood costs by exploring a backward vertical integration strategy, contemplating the acquisition of forestlands in Chile. Application of statistical analysis, optimization techniques, and Excel Solver is utilized to develop a comprehensive aggregate plan for raw lumber production. Three strategies Chase, Level, and Optimal are evaluated to determine the most cost-effective approach. The Optimal Strategy is emerged as the preferred solution, showcasing the effectiveness of leveraging statistical techniques and optimization tools in achieving efficient aggregate planning.
